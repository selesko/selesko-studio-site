Concept SF.003 — The Basalt Wedge

A Shield and a Lens
Location: Mosier, Oregon (Columbia River Gorge)
Typology: Private Residence + Artist Studio
Program: 2,800 sq. ft. (2,400 Main + 400 Studio)
Performance: Passive House Standard (< 0.6 ACH50) · CLT Structure

The Makers

A couple relocating to the Columbia River Gorge not to slow down, but to focus. With a ceramicist in the family, the program required a strict separation between "Living" and "Making." They needed a sanctuary for rest and a durable "machine" for creative work—a dirty studio capable of handling silica dust and kiln heat without compromising indoor air quality.

The home is organized around a ritual commute. The Dirty Studio is detached from the main living volume, accessible only via a conditioned glass bridge—a "Vitrified Passage" that creates a psychological threshold between home and workshop, forcing a moment of suspension over the raw basalt.

Key Features

  • The Shield — A long, linear bar oriented East-West creates a massive wind shadow. The sleeping zone is buffered by a thickened "acoustic wall" while the Great Room opens entirely to the North view.
  • The Bridge — A transparent connector bridges a natural basalt fissure, linking the entry mudroom to the studio.
  • The Satellite Studio — An independent volume angled to capture specific light, acoustically isolated and built for ceramic production.
  • Passive House Envelope — Airtight with triple-glazed curtain walls—the home remains draft-free even during 40mph Gorge gales.
