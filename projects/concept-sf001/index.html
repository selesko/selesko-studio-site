Concept SF.001 — Skidmore Infill

Density Without Compromise
Location: North Portland, OR
Typology: Single Family Infill + ADU
Performance: Passive Building Strategies

The Skinny Lot, Reconsidered

The "skinny lot" is a staple of Portland's densification strategy, but it often results in dark, tunnel-like homes. The Skidmore Infill challenges this typology by treating the 25-foot wide site not as a restriction, but as a vertical aperture.

The design breaks the massing into two distinct volumes separated by a central light-well courtyard—sacrificing a small amount of square footage to gain a massive amount of environmental quality, allowing southern light to penetrate deep into the ground floor while maintaining privacy from the street.

Our strategy pushes the service core tight against the northern property line, creating an unobstructed living bar along the south face. The line of sight extends from the front entry all the way to the rear garden, making the 18-foot width feel double its size.

Key Features

  • The Side-Core Efficiency — By pushing the service core to the northern property line, we create an unobstructed living bar running the full length of the south face.
  • The Light-Well Courtyard — A small outdoor courtyard draws daylight deep into the ground floor—transforming a potential liability into the home's defining spatial moment.
  • The Deep Threshold — A charred cedar (Shou Sugi Ban) rainscreen with deep-set windows eliminates thermal bridges and creates heavy, permanent materiality.
  • Detached ADU — A flexible income unit that mirrors the design language while preserving the main home's privacy.
