Jeff Goldblatt

Architect (AIA) Passive House Certified (CPHB) Living Future Accredited (LFA)

With a background in large-scale sustainable design at firms — where I designed the first Living Building Challenge certified project in Portland — I bring the rigor of commercial practice to the intimacy of residential work.

As a Certified Passive House Builder (CPHB) and Living Future Accredited (LFA) professional certified to design to Passive House standards, sustainability is not just a buzzword — it is part of my DNA.

I believe architecture is a hands-on discipline. Whether detailing a rainscreen assembly or restoring vintage electronics in the shop, I am obsessed with how things go together.

"Selesko"

Selesko is not just my middle name; it is my grandmother's maiden name. To carry the name Selesko is an act of bringing the past forward. It is a reminder that we do not build in a vacuum; we build in a continuum. Just as a home inherits the landscape it sits on, our work inherits the stories that came before. It is a commitment to craft architecture that honors where we come from while sheltering where we are going.
